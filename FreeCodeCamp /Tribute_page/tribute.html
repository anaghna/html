Kalpana Chawla

Kalpana Chawla, the first woman of Indian origin in space.
  • Kalpana Chawla was an Indian-American astronaut and aerospace engineer.
  • She made history as the first woman of Indian descent to travel to space.
  • She was born on March 17, 1962, in Karnal, Haryana, India.
  • She earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from Punjab Engineering College.
  • She moved to the United States in 1982 to pursue higher studies in aerospace engineering.
  • She obtained her Master's degree from the University of Texas at Arlington and a Ph.D. from the University of Colorado Boulder.
  • She began working at NASA's Ames Research Center in 1988, focusing on computational fluid dynamics.
  • In 1994, she was selected as an astronaut candidate by NASA and completed rigorous training.
  • She made her first spaceflight in 1997 aboard Space Shuttle Columbia as a mission specialist.
  • Her second mission was STS-107 in 2003, where she conducted experiments in microgravity and space science.
  • On February 1, 2003, during re-entry into Earth's atmosphere, the Columbia shuttle disintegrated, and all seven crew members, including Kalpana, lost their lives.
  • She was posthumously awarded the Congressional Space Medal of Honor, and several institutions and satellites have been named in her memory.
  • Kalpana remains a symbol of courage, determination, and excellence for aspiring scientists and women worldwide.
